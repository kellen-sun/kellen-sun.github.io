🚀 The Book in 3 Sentences

  1. Save your way to 25000 dollars.
  1. Make your biggest expense into income and find scalable streams of income.
  1. Invest your savings to achieve financial freedom.

🎨 Impressions

My impressions of this book are that it covers a narrow enough topic without delving into huge specifics. This has also led to some degree of repetition by the author. For example, house hacking is mentioned several times, but spotting the right house hack is never explored in depth. This book provides arguments that are easy to agree with and the use of statistics is very convincing. The concept of financial runway though common is a very powerful way to think about money. Additionally, the emphasis on reducing lifestyle costs is shown through countless examples to be advantageous both by reducing the required “assets x returns” as well as building more assets. I like the concept of financial freedom requiring low-risk investments because the money has to stay stable. However, I believe there are advantages to higher-risk investment that aren’t being explored. The book makes a clear distinction between income and assets/wealth. Also, it introduced me to a different view about risk where risk is that of not fully building wealth and not of losing assets. I learned a bit about the cost of missing out where opportunities have benefits of their own and missing them is similar to losing actual money. I believe that all the topics discussed have much more depth and this book serves as a good introduction to them. This book also discussed why others don’t achieve financial freedom and how acting like the norm will not get me to my goal which is why often drastic action is required.

How I Discovered It

I discovered this book while searching for books to improve personal finance. I wanted to start improving my financial worth and googled the best books for that. I believe it was on an Investopedia website.

Who Should Read It?

I would recommend this book to teens to early twenties because this is when you have fewer debts and can start fresh to follow advice. Older people can still follow the book, but they would have to take drastic action and change their lifestyle as well as those around them.

📒 Summary + Notes

Introduction

There are three stages of wealth generation. Characterized by the main action that has to be accomplished in each one. First, one must save to accumulate the first $25,000. Then, one must increase their income to build up to $100,000 in wealth. Finally, one must invest to make enough to sustain one’s lifestyle indefinitely.

Part 1: The First $25,000 Is the Hardest

Places the reader in a position to have 1 year of financial runway. This first meaningful amount of capital will allow one to pursue more opportunities. The short runway already offers more flexibility and freedom as well as security in one’s financial position. The author emphasizes how this amount in cash or equivalents needs to be readily accessible unlike equity in a mortgage or 401(k) retirement plans. This section talked about living on less so that one could save more capital and that for the same amount of capital it would result in a longer financial runway.

Chapter 1: Building The First $25,000 through Frugality

There are 3 steps to achieve financial freedom:

  1. Accumulate real assets that produce income and increase in value
  1. Constantly seek to invest their money effectively
  1. Design a lifestyle that cost as little as possible to maintain

The author explains the importance of frugality.

Frugality: the quality of being economical with money or food; thriftiness.

There are several reasons that frugal living is important to financial freedom.

  1. Frugality enables you to seek and opens up opportunities
  1. Money saved is more valuable than money earned because of taxes

If one constantly wants to achieve financial freedom, then frugality should come naturally. Each dollar spent would be a dollar taken away from their goal towards freedom. Those folks who are serious about their goal will not need to set budgets as they are constantly thinking about their goal. Additionally, people who believe in the power of frugality will tackle problems mostly on their own instead of outsourcing easy problems to professionals. In this day and age, it’s very easy to find information on the internet about common problems that people would spend money on. Replacing a toilet and calling a plumber, feeling slightly off and calling a doctor, oil change and going to the mechanic are only a few of the easy to fix problems people outsource that could save them hundreds of dollars. Obviously, one should hire a professional for tasks not worth their time or tasks that are dangerous. Finally, to live frugally one has to obviously live without the best product every time. They have to skip the fancy restaurants, expensive new phones, and appliances and buy products that are “good enough”. The small costs saved on a daily basis will add up and become significant savings down the line.

Chapter 2: How to Live an Efficient Lifestyle

There are some spending habits that statistically contribute much more than others. For example, housing, transportation, food, insurance and healthcare.

Housing costs can be reduced in many ways. Primarily one must choose to live in a place that costs less. This means living in a house that is just enough for your needs instead of buying the largest home one qualifies for. Housing costs can also be reduced by renting out parts of the house.

Transportation cost is going to be greatly reduced if one lives near their workplace and can bike to work. This means that there is no need to buy a car at all and there is the added benefit to health. Also, if one absolutely needs to buy a car, then a working old car is often better than a new one because the old one would have seen the bulk of its depreciation.

The simplest way to reduce food costs is to eat out less, learn to cook healthy food and prepare it yourself at home. Going out for food is very expensive. Additionally, some grocery stores have much cheaper off-brand products that will significantly reduce the required cost of food. One has to get into the habit of not eating out and eating healthily.

Once one has a good amount saved up they could pay less of a premium in insurance because one would have saved money that could pay for common issues that your insurance might not cover. This way one would have higher deductibles and pay a lower premium.

The easiest way to reduce healthcare costs is to exercise regularly and to keep in good health so that large healthcare bills are not necessary. Exercising, like on the way to work, and eating healthily will benefit one’s health hugely.

The easiest entertainment costs to eliminate are paid TV and subscriptions. Many people will forget to cancel subscriptions which will cost them a lot of money. Additionally, paid TV can be replaced by cheaper alternatives or eliminated completely.

The newest clothing is not necessary for one’s apparel. There are often better options and it is crucial just to avoid waste in this category.

Cash Contributions is a personal choice and one can save money on them as they’re often tax-deductible.

Often education is very expensive compared to learning from books at the library and online courses. Many people will get into huge student loans which are difficult to pay off. Additionally, they’ll buy more expensive houses to have their children attend a slightly better school.

One needs to save as much as possible of one’s income. Saving a few percent is not enough. At least 50% should be saved off your income to make significant strides towards financial freedom. The difference in buying a new SUV versus a new sedan is minimal, but not buying a car at all will have a much larger impact. It’s always more effective to tackle the largest costs first instead of the small expenses as those often also provide more joy.

Chapter 3: What to Do with Money as You Save It

There are three steps of things to do with the saved money. First, the first 1000-2000 saved dollars must immediately go into an emergency fund. This will alleviate a lot of pressure from day-to-day life when in difficulty for money. This cash should only be used in the utmost emergencies, but it might help in reducing premiums on insurance. The money will provide peace of mind in knowing that daily expenses can be covered in cases of losing one’s job, injuries, etc. Second, one should aim to pay down bad debts and improve one’s credit score. Bad debts are those with high-interest rates or that stop one long term without building an asset such as car loan, student loan and credit card debt. Those must be paid back fast in order to not lose more money on them and increase the amount of money that can be saved. Improving credit score is also important because it allows one to borrow more money which can be useful in mortgages and having lower interest rates. Finally, one should aim to build a reserve of easily accessible money as a financial runway. If one saves enough money this should only take a year to build up. It’ll allow one to look out at many other opportunities and not be as reliant on a daytime 9-5 job. This backup cash can be used as a down payment on a property, pursuing a new career with less risk, acquiring meaningful assets or starting a business.

Part II: From $25,000 to $100,000 through Housing and Income Generation

In this part, the author explains how to use one’s $25,000 to help scale to larger incomes with less risk as well as increase one’s income. It’s made clear that the first section is the hardest part and from here on one can utilize their available capital to their advantage to build wealth.

Chapter 4: Turning Your Largest Expense into an Income Producing Asset

This chapter provides a technique called house hacking to reduce one’s housing expense to nearly 0 or even make it an income. There are three ways an average person can house themselves. First, they can rent a property. Second, they can buy the property. Third, they can buy the property live in a part of it and rent out the rest. For example, in a duplex, one can buy it live in one portion and rent the other half. This reduces cost and the renting cost could even cover the mortgage payments. As a matter of fact, house hacking is not only the best way to reduce spending costs, but it also builds net worth fastest. It takes the advantages of renting and buying a house without their consequences which makes this strategy super powerful to save more money. If one is capable of saving half their income and another third from housing costs then they could save 70% maybe every single year of their salary.
