First essay

Introduces the concept that we are all far from ourselves. We do not know ourselves nor do we wish to find ourselves.

The upper class (royalty, monarchy, etc) created the term “good” to describe themselves which subsequently made “bad” for everyone else. The word itself is created in multiple historical instances to describe themselves. Then with the addition of other words to describe the upper class such as pure, truthful, etc. those words got associated into “good” and their opposites into bad. This made the distinct separation between those who are good and those who aren’t.

The priests view of good is much different; they view the miserable, the poor, the powerless, as good.

“From their powerlessness their hatred grows to take on a monstrous and sinister shape, the most cerebral and most poisonous form.” → “Love grew forth from this hatred”
