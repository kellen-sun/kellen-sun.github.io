“There is a theory which states that if ever anyone discovers exactly what the Universe is for and why it is here, it will instantly disappear and be replaced by something even more bizarre and inexplicable. There is another which states that this has already happened.”

How do we know that what we know is actually true? Science is a process of making our best effort to know what’s really real.

Neuropsychological Humility and Mechanisms of Deception

Memories are altered to fit internal narratives that can change over time. They change by pressures of social norms and can even be entirely created through suggestion and hypnosis. We tend to personalize memories of others so that they occur to ourselves and to add details that people suggest to us.

Our senses are way worse than we think. We can only see a small percentage of things, have blind spots, then our brain fills in the details of what it expects to see and blurs everything that we don’t focus on. We use previous expectations to reconstruct what we see. About 50% of people won’t notice when the person they speak to suddenly switches, same for a sudden color change in a video or if a gorilla walks on backstage while they’re focusing on the play.

Pareidolia: Perceiving images in random noise when there’s actually nothing. What we see looks like something so our brain matches it to it and then fills in the necessary details. This works for hearing too. Our brains are especially good at seeing faces due to its evolutionary need to quickly recognize facial expressions.

Hyperactive Agency Detection is when our brains are deeply wired to find the difference between agents and objects. Those who can act consciously and those who can’t. When we see unusual patterns of motion or coincidences we assume that an external agent is at work when in most cases shit just happens.

Hypnagogia: A neurological phenomenon in which the dreaming and waking states are fused. Likely to happen when waking up or falling asleep and the brain glitches giving us sleep paralysis that occurs during REM sleep while being awake. This gives a feeling of being paralyzed by an outside source. In fact the phenomenon is not a reflection of external reality but rather an internal experience happening in the brain.

Ideomotor effect is an involuntary subconscious subtle muscle movement driven by expectation which creates the illusion that the movement is due to an external force.

Metacognition

Dunning-Kruger effect describes the inability to evaluate one’s own competency. Incompetent people cannot recognize how incompetent they are and often act with extra confidence. Ignorance carries with it the inability to accurately assess one’s own ignorance.

Motivated reasoning is the biased process we use to defend a position, ideology or belief that we hold with emotional investment. We take in information with biases and some we want to win while others we want to lose.

“People will try to adjust their beliefs to simultaneously maximize the alignment of those beliefs with facts and maximize their emotional comfort.”

A logical fallacy is an invalid proof of an argument from a set of starting facts

“I am convinced that the act of thinking logically cannot possibly be natural to the human mind. If it were, then mathematics would be everybody’s easiest course at school and our species would not have taken several millennia to figure out the scientific method.” -Neil deGrasse Tyson

Arguments are discussions that involve attempts at convincing others. Basically just logic. First examine your own argument and premises.

Biases is to think in a way to favor certain information and prefer some conclusions over others

Heuristics are mental shortcuts to save time and compute power.

We would do the same as everyone else if we were in their shoes and had their backgrounds and experiences. So we should criticize them less for their behaviours because we are all human

Anomaly hunting: Is actively trying to find anomalies and then instead of explaining them with prior theories you create new theories to explain them. Additionally, instead of considering the odds of an anomaly happening people consider the odds that the specific anomaly occurred.

Data mining is when we search for patterns when none actually occur. We try to make some theory about a specific pattern happening when given our large datasets, a pattern is bound to occur.

Science and Pseudoscience

Science is about what we can investigate. If we can’t investigate it in the scientific manner then it isn’t science.

“Each scientific discipline is not a world unto itself, but part of the overall scientific endeavor to explain reality. Since all science is exploring the same reality, it all has to agree.”

“Occam’s razor states that when two or more hypotheses are consistent with the available data, then the hypothesis that introduces the fewest new assumptions should be preferred.”

“Science requires courage—the courage to face reality and accept its findings, even if they upset us or are disruptive to our comfortable ideology.”

Conspiracy theorists combine many fronts of faulty reasoning to construct an improbable description of reality and defending it. They only take evidence that benefits them, doesn’t make something testable, argue our motives if we disagree, interpret data in ways that favours them.

Anecdotes are a weak form of evidence and should only be used to generate hypotheses rather than confirming them.

Iconic Cautionary Tales from History

“Those who do not learn history are doomed to repeat it.”

People who defy science and think they made a new invention through pseudoscience often lack humility. A true scientist would not believe that they broke the law of conservation of energy and would instead present their idea as something odd that needs more investigating while a pseudoscientist would claim having a source of infinite energy (they think they’re a genius).

The idea of intelligent design; that a creator made the universe is unfalsifiable and therefore not scientific.

“There will always be gaps in our scientific knowledge, and their presence doesn’t really tell us anything about the power and success of a scientific explanatory paradigm. A better method of assessment is to look at how it has progressed.” (first derivative with respect to time)

Adventures in Skepticism

To get information: Check the source, try to use a variety of source, track the information back to the original source, look for a variety of opinions, independently examine the arguments of each side, talk with respected experts, don’t accept the answers you want.

“All conclusions are tentative”

Recognize ideological positions: “Ideological positions tend to be rigid. They settle on heir conclusions and then search for justification, and they will change their justification as needed but never question their conclusion”

Talks about 9/11 conspiracies and GMO conspiracies

Skepticism and the Media

Not all news outlet report facts and many fall on the spectrum towards fake news.

Echo chambers: when viewpoints are echoed within a group of people who have the same viewpoints. We seek information that aligns with our own.

When a scientific fact is mostly confirmed diverging viewpoints should get less time of presentation of their viewpoints because it’s “wasteful” and doesn’t serve much purpose other than misinformation. Ie: Why is climate change activists and deniers given an equal say when the data clearly shows one direction.

Science news articles are often deformed in order to make it more interesting.

Death by Pseudoscience

Skeptics don’t really care about people’s believes but when they try to state something or draw a conclusion from those beliefs then they should be held to the rules of science.

Denying the science can lead to disastrous consequences. For example, in South Africa, their president between 1999 and 2008 denied the HIV and didn’t provide appropriate cure for his people. This led to 330,000 premature deaths.
