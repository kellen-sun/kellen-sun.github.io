🚀 The Book in 3 Sentences

  1. Invest in your assets first and grow those to pay for your expenses.
  1. Learn because your mind is your greatest asset so invest in it.
  1. Become financially literate so that you can use corporations and pay less taxes and such.

🎨 Impressions

Robert writes (often in first person) confidently and sometimes what may appear to be arrogance. This confidence is well received as it conveys his message stronger and makes the reader believe in his lessons to becoming rich more. He pulls from his personal life as an appeal to authority as he walks the reader through countless specific examples of how his rules can be applied.

How I Discovered It

It’s a pretty popular book in personal finance. Not hard to come across in instagram posts, youtube videos, and list rankings.

Who Should Read It?

Everyone in my opinion. If you care about leaving the rat race and making some real money and fixing your financial situation, it’s a must. It teaches other skills too, but is heavily applicable and useful for finances. Should be a read for young people beginning their finances.

☘️ How the Book Changed Me

  • Taught me ideas about finances such as using a corporation properly and investing in assets
  • Learned that hands on learning is much more important and that learning through life in general is crucial for success
  • It made me decide that I actually have pretty bad financial literacy.

📒 Summary + Notes

Introduction

Financial mindset is taught at home not in school and thus parents who are already poor are teaching their kids money skills. Their kids trust their parents and end up also having poor people financial skills.

It’s important to think for oneself.

It’s important to mentally exercise to increase your financial mindset and your wealth. (Similar to exercising in the gym) Keep asking yourself financial questions and stuff.

“People really do shape their lives through their thoughts.”

“I’m a rich man, and rich people don’t do this”

“There is a difference between being poor and being broke. Broke is temporary. Poor is eternal.”

“I don’t work for money! Money works for me!”

Often taking the road less traveled by when two roads diverged makes all the difference.

Lesson 1: The Rich don’t Work for Money

The poor and the middle class work for money.
The rich have money work for them.

You have to be able to make decisions fast in business and finance or else you risk losing opportunities.

“Life pushes all of us around. Some people give up and others fight. A few learn the lesson and move on. They welcome life pushing them around. To these few people, it means they need and want to learn something. They learn and move on.”

Stop wanting to change others, “it’s easier to change yourself than everyone else.”

“Passion is anger and love combined.”

Many people are not directed by their passion to make money, but by their fear of losing it.

People are stuck in the rat race because of fear and greed.

“Instead of admitting the truth about how they feel, they react to their feelings and fail to think.”

You need to choose your thoughts focusing on the long term.

“Stop blaming me and thinking I’m the problem. If you think I’m the problem, then you have to change me. If you realize that you’re the problem, then you can change yourself, learn something and grow wiser.”

Lesson 2: Why Teach Financial Literacy?

It’s not how much money you make.
It’s how much money you keep.

Money doesn’t solve problems, intelligence does.

“You must know the difference between an asset and a liability, and buy assets.”

KISS principle: Keep it Super Simple.

An asset puts money into your pocket. A liability takes money out of your pocket.

The rich buy assets, the poor and middle class buy liabilities thinking they are assets.

If people struggle financially, they usually are illiterate financially.

“It is the cash flow that shows the story of how a person handles his or her money”

“Remember the Golden Rule. He who has the gold makes the rules.”

“most people follow the crowd. They do things because everybody else does them. They conform, rather than question.” As the world population grows and social media rises, the crowd becomes larger, easier to follow and harder to question. Those who question the crowd come in such large numbers that they form their own crowd sometimes undermining the purpose of their uprising.

“when it comes to money, high emotions tend to lower financial intelligence. I know from personal experience that money has a way of making every decision emotional”

A home is not an asset but it’s a liability, because it takes money out of your pocket. It requires expenses, to pay of mortgage, taxes, insurance, and utilities. While it doesn’t bring you any income through rent. The cashflow is negative. Additionally, the cost of paying for a house leads to missed opportunity where that money could’ve been invested in assets.

Wealth is the number of days one can survive without working. (So you only take income from assets, and deduce them to your expenses)

Lesson 3: Mind your own Business

The rich focus on their asset columns
while everyone else focuses on their income statements.

Many people after a whole day of work basically get nothing for themselves. No light at the end of the tunnel.

“Your business revolves around your asset column.”

Your profession revolves around your income column (from salaries)

“Once a dollar goes into your asset column, it becomes your employee.”

The rich buy luxuries last paying for it using the income generated from their assets. The poor buy luxuries on credit trying to appear rich.

To mind your business means working for yourself not your employer, the government (taxes) or others.

Lesson 4: The History of Taxes and the Power of Corporations

My rich dad just played the game smart, and he did it through corporations - the biggest secret of the rich

Corporations allow one to pay for some expenses with pre-tax money and they have lower income taxes. By creating taxes the government merely became greedy in its spending of money (always being valued for spending more and more and higher more people). This lead them to tax lower and lower income brackets. Meanwhile, the rich who are financially literate get taxes less because they outsmart the government officials (who often times are not financially literate) and that’s what makes them rich in the first place. Rich people’s ability to not get tricked by taxes is legal in all manners.

The price of taxes is working from January to May for the government.

The rich play the game smarter (due to their financial literacy)

Knowledge is power, “without that knowledge the world pushes you around.”

Having money work for you instead of working for money means you keep the power instead of giving it to your employer.

Robert’s poor dad spoke of studying hard and working hard to climb the corporate ladder (which would make him a docile cow ready for milking). But his rich dad asked, “why not own the ladder?”

“Each dollar in my asset column was a great employee, working hard to make more employees.”

Corporations allow you to earn then spend with pretax money then get taxed. (ie: you can buy a Porsche with your corporation as an expense for travel or delivery, have board meetings in Hawaii, buy mansions as investment properties, etc)

While as an employee you get taxed before even having the money in your hands.

Lesson 5: The Rich Invent Money

Often in the real world, it’s not the smart who get ahead, but the bold.

Everyone has a bold personality and a scared, doubting personality and they’re both good in their own ways. Often the one that shows more dictates the way we act and control risks.

Many people fail to notice that they are the problem and spend too much time blaming others for problems instead of working on themselves/ seeing what they could do different.

“Old ideas are their biggest liability. It is a liability simply because they fail to realize that while that idea or way of doing something was an asset yesterday, yesterday is gone.”

“Few people realize that luck is created, just as money is.”

Our most powerful asset is our mind. As an asset it should bring money into the income statement.

Money is not real, it is “what we agree it is”

“What is risky for one person is less risky for someone else.”

When someone said “You can’t do that here” Robert replied with “I don’t know how to do that here — yet” Showing his differing mindset.

We as humans are designed to learn by making mistakes, we learn to walk by falling down, (learn handstands through failures). This is not what school teaches us and it certainly is however the way to learn to make money later in life.

“Winners are not afraid of losing. But losers are. Failure is part of the process of success. People who avoid failure also avoid success.”

2 types of investors:

  1. Buys an off the shelf investment, mutual fund, real estates, bonds, etc (like buying a computer)
  1. Creates investments by assembling opportunities together. (like building a computer)

To be the second:

  1. Find an opportunity that everyone else missed.
  1. Raise money. (Not just at the bank)
  1. Organize smart people.

Lesson 6: Work to Learn — Don’t Work for Money

Robert and his wife pick books with an advisor each year to read and discuss together in order to learn more.

By working in a place where you can learn you’ll learn by picking up jargon and stuff through osmosis. Ie be in an accounting department for a bit

Crucial to know a bit about a lot

“Workers work hard enough to not be fired, and owners pay just enough so that workers won’t quit”

Seek work for what you learn rather than what you earn.

The more specialized you are the more dependent you are on that specialty.

Main skills to learn are

  1. Management of cash flow
  1. Management of systems
  1. Management of people
  1. Sales (Communications)
  1. Marketing

Sales and marketing also teaches you to overcome your fear of rejection

Chapter 7: Overcoming Obstacles

The primary difference between a rich person and a poor person is how they manage fear.

The five obstacles are

  1. Fear
  1. Cynicism
  1. Laziness
  1. Bad habits
  1. Arrogance

“If you’re going to go broke, go big.”

No one has ridden a bike without falling down first, no golfer has never lost a gold ball, no one has ever fallen in love without being heartbroken. And no one has ever made money without losing money.

For most people, the pain of losing money is greater than the joy of winning.

A bit of greed for your time will make you more time and won’t have you complaining about being too lazy.

Lesson 8: Getting Started

First, find a reason greater than reality. Identify what you don’t wanna do in life and from those create the strong desires for what you want.

Realize that your daily habits add up and lead you to where you end up. If you spend like a poor person, then you’ll end up poor and so on.

Stop avoiding your main goal. Admit to yourself that you want, face the goal/task and get it fucking done. There’s no point denying it.

Make the daily choices that will get you to your goal.

You learn from all your friends so care much about who you befriend.

Learn a lot and a lot and repeat because what you know is what you become. Learn different ways to handle money, investments, etc. Learn formulas, methods of doing stuff and try them to see if they work.

Pay yourself first, this makes you self-disciplined. The world pushes you around and pressures you. The rich can resist the pressure. This also means investing into asset columns first before paying bills

Find good brokers, because they make you more money and save your time. Their knowledge and information is worth the price they charge.

Give. Give to receive. And give to others. Be generous in your giving.

Use assets to buy luxuries

Have heroes, and people you look up to. Learn from them as they inspire you.

Teach and you will receive, because giving will return.
