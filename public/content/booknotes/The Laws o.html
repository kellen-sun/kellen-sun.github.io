1: Master Your Emotional Self; The Law of Irrationality

We are all consumed by something, but Pericles has decided to be consumed by intelligence and rationality.

To be rational you often have to retreat yourself from the situation and think deeply in order for your emotions to wash away. You have to question why you have the emotions you do that are making you do the things they are. Why are you angry, sad, etc? and only then can you start questioning the situation itself rationally.

Humans are fundamentally not rational, which must be understood to become rational

The brain structure itself reveals this with older parts being for emotion that leads to direct action instead (this part of the brain is for deep desires, animalistic wants and needs, survival, etc...) of the thought and rationality that the newer part of the brain has.

Our emotions are thus hidden from our rational self. The inability for humans to transfer emotions and language between the two parts of the brain creates more friction between them making the emotions less precise, and last longer.

The most common emotion is just desiring pleasure and avoiding pain. (helps satisfy our egos)

Confirmation bias: searching for evidence to support our points and discrediting evidence against our points. We should instead search for arguments against our ideas and contemplate them as objectively as possible. This often happens when asking for advice, (only taking the advice we want to hear)

Conviction bias: Those who speak like the know a lot, and are very convinced of what they say are trusted more. (think advertisements, leaders, politics, etc)

Appearance bias: we don‚Äôt see people as they are but as they appear. People usually show the best of themselves thus we are biased in seeing that part of them. And this can also create the halo effect where if a person has some quality we assume they also have some other quality.

Group bias: as social animals we follow the group while thinking that our ideas are our own to satisfy our ego when many members of the group will do similarly.

Blame bias: it‚Äôs painful to relive mistakes, so we blame them on someone else while thinking that we learn from our mistakes.

Superiority bias: we can‚Äôt see our own faults and biases only those of others. we think of ourselves as more rational, ethical, etc. This is partly because if we think so otherwise and show that we do not think of ourselves as good then we are put at a disadvantage in society.

Love your rationality so that it can be easy to aim for.

Search for the roots of your emotions.

6: Elevate Your Perspective; The Law of Shortsightedness

By waiting and allowing time to pass we get a much better perspective on things. It is the human nature to rush and live in the moment, seeking instant gratification. This only got worse with the advent of recent technologies, (social media, internet, etc.)

However, we often cannot wait for time to pass by and must get something done while still doing it well. It‚Äôs important to have time/perspective to do something well. Considering more scenarios other than the one we want and hope for will often makes us realize flaws in our plans. Often we are just blinded by a single possibility and we take action firmly believing that will happen. Also look longer down the line a few years.

Additionally, when setting goals and future plans make sure they are concrete this shows that you are connecting fully with the future. Slow down when you have to make some more important decisions instead of acting directly, taking in a few days to think more this will allow decisions to be much better. Don‚Äôt always think about those one scenarios but of multiple. See the passage of time as an ally something that helps you not something that you are losing.
