RULE 1: Stand Up Straight With Your Shoulders Back

All animals develop a social hierarchy, where those at the top are more likely to survive and mate. This social hierarchy is often related to the environment or habitat. Some places are better habitats to live in while others are worse. Those in the better places (more shelter, food, etc) have a better chance of surviving.

Things aren’t equally distributed in populations. Examples include money where the top 1% own more than the bottom 50%. The same applies to other resources. Additionally, this applies to scientific papers, a small group of scientists write the vast majority of papers and a small group of composers (Mozart, Bach, Beethoven, Tchaikovsky) wrote the vast majority of classical music. Even more surprisingly, a small number of pieces written by these composers make up the vast majority of their music played. The same concept of unequal distribution applies to other populations (animals) and things.

Even a third of a billion years ago, the neural chemicals to process society and status had developed in animals’ brains.

“There is very little difference between the capacity for mayhem and destruction, integrated, and strength of character.”

“To stand up straight with your shoulders back is to accept the terrible responsibility of life, with eyes wide open. voluntarily accepting the burden of Being. You step forward to take your place in the dominance hierarchy. Thus emboldened, you will embark on the voyage of your life, let your light shine, so to speak, on the heavenly hill, and pursue your rightful destiny. Then the meaning of your life may be sufficient to keep the corrupting influence of mortal despair at bay. May be able to accept the terrible burden of the World, and find joy.”
